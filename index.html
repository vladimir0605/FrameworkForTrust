00.00 — THE PROBLEM

Every day, millions of real-world events happen around us — traffic incidents, floods, outages, local disruptions. This information exists, but it's scattered across closed platforms, unverifiable, and impossible to trust at scale.

There is no shared, neutral layer that says: this happened, here, and it's been verified — without relying on a single company or government to vouch for it.

FfT builds that layer directly into geography itself, dividing the planet into ownable, mintable quadrants that anchor events to place and time, permanently and verifiably.

01.00 — ARCHITECTURE

Four primitives

Everything in the protocol is built from a small set of composable parts.

A

Geo-Quadrants

The Earth's surface divided into a grid of ownable cells, minted as NFTs. Each quadrant is a fixed coordinate anchor that events attach to.

B

Trust Events

Real-world occurrences logged against a quadrant, timestamped, and cryptographically anchored on-chain — tamper-evident by design.

C

GCD Token

The protocol's trust-weighted asset. Stake, earn, or lose reputation based on the accuracy of what you report and verify.

D

Semantic Search

An AI-powered discovery layer over the anchored event graph — query by meaning, not just by coordinates or keywords.
